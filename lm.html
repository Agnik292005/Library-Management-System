Branch Locations

The Grand Reading Hall

Established in 1895, this magnificent hall features soaring 40-foot ceilings adorned with hand-painted murals. The original oak shelving still houses our rare manuscript collection, while modern climate control ensures preservation for future generations. Over 2 million scholars have studied within these hallowed walls.

The Heritage Wing

Built in 1902 as an expansion to accommodate the growing collection, the Heritage Wing showcases Beaux-Arts architecture with its distinctive copper dome. Home to over 50,000 first editions and the city's most extensive genealogy archive, it remains a testament to early 20th-century craftsmanship.

The Victorian Archives

Originally a private collection donated in 1888, this section preserves the literary legacy of the Gilded Age. The restored gas lamp fixtures now house LED bulbs, maintaining historical ambiance while protecting delicate materials. It contains the region's largest collection of 19th-century periodicals.
